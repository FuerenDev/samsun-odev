Samsun
Türkiye  ·  Black Sea  ·  City of Liberation
About Samsun
Samsun is a major city on the Black Sea coast of northern Türkiye. With a population exceeding 1.3 million, it is a vibrant port city and a key economic hub. Samsun holds a special place in Turkish history as the location where the War of Independence began in 1919. It perfectly blends coastal beauty, rich maritime culture, and a dynamic urban lifestyle.
Extra Info
Samsun enjoys a mild, humid Black Sea climate with lush green landscapes and frequent rainfall. It is strategically positioned as a gateway to the Black Sea region. The city features a long, scenic coastline, extensive parks, and modern infrastructure. Its unique geography, situated between mountains and the sea, provides a distinct and refreshing atmosphere compared to Central Anatolia.
Interesting Facts
  • Samsun is where Mustafa Kemal Atatürk landed on May 19, 1919, marking the start of the national movement.
  • It is home to the Bandırma Ferry, a symbol of the Turkish War of Independence.
  • The city hosts the largest statue of Atatürk in Türkiye.
  • Samsun is renowned for its fertile agricultural land, particularly for tobacco and hazelnuts.
  • It serves as one of the most important centers for trade and logistics in the Black Sea region.
Cuisine
The most famous local dish is Bafra pidesi, a long, thin, and crispy pita bread filled with minced meat. The region's cuisine is heavily influenced by the Black Sea, featuring fresh fish like hamsi (anchovy). Other local delicacies include tirit (bread-based meat dish), various corn-based breads, and delicious hazelnut-based desserts.
History
Known in ancient times as Amisos, the city has a long history as a Greek colony and trade outpost. Throughout the ages, it has been ruled by Pontic, Roman, Byzantine, and Seljuk civilizations. Its modern significance was cemented during the early 20th century, as it became the cradle of the movement that founded the Republic of Türkiye.
